What is Chiropractic? As a profession, chiropractic has been in existence since 1898. According to the Rubicon group, Chiropractic is a health-care profession based on a neo-vitalistic philosophy due to its natural focus on health and wellness, exluding drugs and surgery. There are approximately 100.000 chiropractors worldwide, educated in over 40 universities, where students graduate after a minimum of 5 years of studies. What is a Chiropractic Subluxation? The term subluxation is currently defined as “…a self-perpetuating, central segmental motor control problem that involves a joint, such as a vertebral motion segment, that is not moving appropriately, resulting in ongoing maladaptive neural plastic changes that interfere with the central nervous system’s ability to self-regulate, self-organize, adapt, repair and heal” (The Rubicon Group, May 2017). Reference to original article: https://www.therubicongroup.org/policies/ Chiropractors don’t just adjust anywhere in the spine. Before adjusting you, the Chiroractor will have carried out a thorough exploration, which includes: – Health questionnaire, including previous health history of the patient; – Evaluation of movement / biomechanics of the spine and other joints; Orthopaedic testing; – Chiropractic testing (postural tests, kinesiology tests); – Superficial electromyology testing (Myovision SEMG), which measures electrical activity in the spinal muscles, and thereby evaluates dysfunctional patterns; – Thermography tests (Nervoscope), measuring temperature (inflammation) on both sides of the spine to discover discrepancies; – Sometimes you may be asked for x-rays, MRI’s or other imaging, blood tests, to be able to have a more complete medical history. When a spinal segment doesn’t move correctly, ie., a vertebral subluxation, this can really influence the way the brain perceives and responds to all the sensorial information. Subluxations can have different types of components: biomechanical, neurological, trophic and psicosocial. Our habits and lifestyle are usually the origin of subluxations. Physical stress: bad postures, repeated strain, falls or accidents; chemical factors: hormonal inbalance, alcohol, tabacco, refined food or prepared food; and emotional factors: stress, arguments with your spouse/boss, loss of a loved one, etc; all of these things can affect our balance and wellness, giving rise to subluxations.
